What we do

The bottleneck to every
RWA issuance is legal structuring.

Every founder launching a tokenized asset faces the same first question: how do we structure this to be compliant in our target markets?

Law firms charge hundreds per hour and take weeks. Generic compliance tools are built for post-issuance monitoring, not pre-issuance structuring. There is no affordable, fast alternative.

  • 01
    Legal fees are prohibitive at ideation stage A typical structuring engagement costs tens of thousands before you know if the project is viable.
  • 02
    No unified global framework exists The same token can be a security in the US, a transferable security in the EU, and a capital markets product in Singapore, each with entirely different requirements.
  • 03
    Cross-border structuring has no single playbook A tokenized fund seeking investors across three jurisdictions requires knowledge that no generalist lawyer holds end-to-end.
  • 04
    Token standard choice carries compliance risk Choosing the wrong standard for the wrong jurisdiction creates legal exposure that surfaces only at issuance, too late to fix cheaply.
  • 05
    Regulations change faster than legal advice MiCA, GENIUS Act, MAS guidelines, the landscape shifts constantly. Static legal memos go stale within months.

The engine

Five inputs.
One complete report.

Enter your asset type, target jurisdictions, investor profile, token standard preference, and current project status. The engine processes everything and returns a structured, actionable report you can share with co-founders, investors, and counsel.

Output 01
Legal Entity Recommendation
SPV, RAIF, Cayman trust, Singapore VCC, the right structure for your asset and markets, with rationale.
Output 02
Token Classification Analysis
Security, e-money token, asset-referenced token, or utility token, per jurisdiction, with the exact regulatory basis.
Output 03
Licenses and Registrations
Which licenses you need, which authority to contact, and approximate timeline, per market, not as a generic list.
Output 04
Documentation Checklist
Offering memorandum, subscription agreement, KIID, AML/KYC policy, what you need and in what order.
Output 05
Smart Contract Requirements
Which transfer restrictions and investor whitelist logic must be encoded, matched to your chosen token standard.
Output 06
Risk Flags and Next Steps
Red zones, mitigation options, and an ordered action list distinguishing what requires a lawyer from what does not.
